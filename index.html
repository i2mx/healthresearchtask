What is diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic long-lasting health condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. The body breaks food down into glucose and releases it into the bloodstream. When blood sugars rise your pancreas is supposed to release insulin. Which tells the cells in your body to let in the glucose and turn it into energy.

People with diabetes don't have enough insulin in their bodies or can't use the insulin as well as it should.

Health Effects

When the body doesn't have enough insulin, the blood sugars will rise; Over time, this can cause serious health problems, such as heart disease, vision loss, kidney disease and death.

Symptoms

The symptoms of diabetes develop gradually over years; Some poeple don't notice any symptoms at all. Symptoms include thirst, frequent urination, hunger, fatigue, constantly being tired, poor wound healing and blurred vision. In some cases, there may be no symptoms at all.

Risk Factors

You are at a higher risk for developing type 2 diabetes if you:

  • Have a higher than normal blood sugar
  • Are overweight
  • Have family members with type 2 diabetes
  • Are 45 years or older
  • Are physically active less than 3 times a week
  • Have ever had gestational diabetes (diabetes during pregnancy) or given birth to a baby who weighed more than 9 pounds
  • If you have non-alcoholic fatty liver disease you may also be at risk for type 2 diabetes.

Treated, Cure and Management

There is no cure for type 2 diabetes, it is something that one will have to live with for the rest of their lives and diabetics will need to take insulin supplements for the rest of their lives. Diabetes is not a fun thing, it can be extremely serious and it is good to try to limit the risk of getting Type 2 Diabetes by avoiding risk factors.

Prevention

You can reduce you're risks of getting diabetes by:

  • Reduce the amout of sweets and sugary food you eat
  • Eating a balanced Diet
  • Excercising Reguarly
  • Staying away from cigarettes
  • Getting your blood tested regularly
